# Delivery & Handover Template

Covers two related moments in a client's lifecycle: the handover when a build goes live, and
offboarding if/when the engagement ends. Both are "end of a stage" documents, which is why they
live together.

---

## Part 1 — Delivery Handover (send at go-live)

```
# Your System Is Live — [Business Name]

Hi [Owner Name],

Your [system name] is live as of [date]. Here's what it does and how to use it day-to-day.

## What's Live
- [Capability 1, in plain language]
- [Capability 2]
- [Capability 3]

## What You Need To Do
- Nothing, day-to-day — it runs automatically.
- You'll get a [SMS/email] notification whenever a new lead comes in, with their details.
- [Any manual step they DO need to do, e.g. "reply to the notification if you want the system
  to know you've handled it"]

## What To Do If Something Looks Wrong
Message/call James directly: [contact]. Response time: [e.g. same business day].

## Your First Report
You'll get your first monthly report on [date] — see client-reporting-template.md for the format.

## Support
Included in your $[X]/month: monitoring, bug fixes, minor wording tweaks.
Not included: new features/channels (quoted separately as a change request).
```

## Part 2 — Offboarding (if/when the engagement ends)

```
# Offboarding — [Business Name]
Termination effective: [date] | Reason (optional, for internal notes): [reason]

## Steps
- [ ] Confirm termination date and final invoice with client (per services agreement notice period)
- [ ] Disconnect the system from the client's live contact channels
- [ ] Export/return any client data held (existing-customer list, message history) — client's data,
      client's to keep
- [ ] Delete client-specific configuration from the build once confirmed no longer needed
- [ ] Final invoice sent and settled
- [ ] Ask (once, no pressure) for a testimonial or reason for leaving — useful either way
- [ ] Archive the client's folder rather than delete it — keeps the history for future reference
```

## Notes
- The handover message is the client's proof that they got what they paid for — keep it concrete
  ("here's what it does") not just a status update ("it's done").
- Offboarding should never feel adversarial, even if the client is leaving on bad terms — a clean
  offboarding is what turns a lost client into a referral instead of a bad review.
