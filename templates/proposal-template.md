# Proposal Template

Send this after the discovery call or AI audit, once the opportunity is clear. Keep it short - one page, scannable, no jargon. The audit report (if one was done) already built the case; this document just turns it into a scoped offer with a price and a yes/no decision.

---

## Proposal Document Structure

```
# Proposal - [Business Name]
Prepared for [Owner Name] by James Heathcote | [Date]

## What We Found
[2-3 sentences summarizing the audit/discovery call - the gap, in their words where possible]

## What's Recommended
**[Package Name - e.g. AI Lead Response + Follow-Up System]**

- [Capability 1 - e.g. auto-replies to new enquiries within 60 seconds]
- [Capability 2 - e.g. qualifies new leads, recognizes existing customers]
- [Capability 3 - e.g. notifies you immediately with lead details]
- [Capability 4 - e.g. automatic 24hr follow-up if no reply]

## What It Costs
| | |
|---|---|
| Setup (one-time) | $[amount] |
| Ongoing (monthly) | $[amount]/month |

Setup covers system build, integration with your existing enquiry channels, and testing.
The monthly fee covers monitoring, maintenance, and updates.

## Timeline
- Signed agreement + intake form: [date]
- Build + testing: [X business days]
- Live: [date]

## Next Step
If this looks good, next step is a short services agreement (attached/linked) and a
15-minute intake call to get the system built around your business specifically.
```

## Notes
- Anchor the price to a concrete outcome where possible ("one extra job a month more than covers this")
- Don't over-negotiate on price in the document itself - if they push back, that's a conversation, not a rewrite
- Keep the proposal itself free of legal language - that's what the services agreement is for
