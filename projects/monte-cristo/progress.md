## Patterns / Weak Points Identified
- **Freeze compounds across multiple windows, not just the first one.** Two incidents now, same setting (public transit, passing moment), same root cause (nervous-system freeze, not a knowledge or awareness gap — the signal is being read correctly both times). The second incident adds a new data point: a closing remark from the other person ("oh, bye") is a real second window, and the freeze can repeat on it once the first one's been missed, because the internal story becomes "it's already over."
- **Verbal freeze under genuine uncertainty ("I don't know" / "idk").** Distinct from the physical freeze — physical read was fine both times, but no words came. Fix that worked immediately both times: name the actual object/event in front of you (the towel, her "third one this cracked" line) rather than reaching for a new idea from nothing. Every clean rep in the 2026-07-24 drill used something real in the room as the anchor.
- **Stakes-linked filler ("um," "you know," "yeah" lead-ins).** Confirmed three times in one session, escalating with stakes: rooftop bar (unfamiliar crowd) → engagement party group intro → groom's mother specifically (highest-status person in the room). Every low-stakes rep in between was clean, and the fix (cut the lead-in, deliver flat) worked immediately when applied — this is a state-dependent tell, not a fixed habit, and it specifically escalates with senior/appraising figures, not just crowd size.
- **New: minimizing real accomplishments in front of senior/appraising people ("not much," "nothing exciting").** Surfaced once (groom's mother scenario) alongside the filler — downplaying something genuinely solid reads as uncertainty about your own value. Added to `curriculum/social-scripts.md`.
