# Story Bank

Real material, tightened for actual delivery — written to be told out loud, not read. Each entry keeps the raw version for reference, the polished version to actually use, and what changed between them.

## Kyoto blackout

**Raw:**
"We went clubbing in Kyoto for first time since we got to japan probably was like 3 weeks in, and we had such a good day, then we went and pre up at hostel with the toxic 196 massive cans. then walked ages to this club and my mate was talking to the so called dj he got us free shots and the next minute I woke up in some alley way looking back its funny but in the moment it wasnt."

**Polished:**
"First proper night out in Kyoto — three weeks into the trip. Pre-gamed at the hostel on those 196 cans, which should honestly be classified as a war crime. Walked what felt like forty minutes to the club, and my mate spent the whole queue buttering up the self-proclaimed DJ, who rewarded him with free shots. Next thing I remember, I was waking up in an alleyway. Never did find out how I got there."

**What changed:**
- Cut "we had such a good day" — throat-clearing, delays the turn
- Cut "looking back it's funny but in the moment it wasn't" — explains the joke instead of trusting it; the gap in memory *is* the punchline
- Kept "self-proclaimed DJ" untouched — the best line in the original, pure self-aware skepticism
- "War crime" for the 196 cans — exaggeration instead of flat description, funnier and still true

## The weight cut

**Raw:**
"For my first fight I got back from Melbourne and I had 5 days to lose 5kg, first day skipped for 1hr and half went on 7km run weight didnt change, then drank nothing much for the rest got beat up on the tuesday at sparring had 3 teaspoons tuna that day and full day of work. and then last day I had to sauna and hot bath got out super light headed put tracksuits on somehow got down to weight for fight day and knocked the guy out first round"

**Polished:**
"First fight was booked five days after I landed back from Melbourne, and I still had five kilos to lose. Day one: skipped rope for an hour, ran seven K. Weight didn't move. So it was basically just water for the rest of the week. Tuesday I got put through sparring anyway, ate three teaspoons of tuna that day, worked a full shift. Last day — sauna, then a hot bath, walked out lightheaded, threw the tracksuit on, somehow made weight. Fight night, I knocked the guy out in the first round."

**What changed:**
- Broke the training grind into short fragments ("Day one:", "Weight didn't move.") — matches how it'd actually be told out loud, speeds the pace through the middle so it doesn't drag before the payoff
- Kept every specific number — 5 days, 5kg, 7K, 3 teaspoons — those are what make it land; a vaguer "barely ate anything" loses the impact
- Stopped cold on "knocked the guy out in the first round" — no "and it felt amazing" tacked on after. The suffering already described is the setup; adding more after the KO steps on the punchline
