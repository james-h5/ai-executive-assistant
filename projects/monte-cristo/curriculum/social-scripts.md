# Social Scripts

A growing reference of real and hypothetical situations, each broken down into what happened/might happen and the best response. New entries get appended at the bottom by the `/monte-cristo` skill — read top to bottom for the starter set, newest entries accumulate below.

---

## Starter Scenarios

### Public transit / passing moment (the train, 2026-07-23)
**Situation:** A stranger smiles at you in a passing, low-stakes environment (train, street, elevator) — no natural reason yet to talk.
**Best response:** Hold eye contact half a beat longer than instinct, return the smile genuinely (eyes engaged, not just mouth). That alone is a complete, low-risk move — it doesn't obligate either person to anything further. If there's a natural next window (same stop, sitting nearby, waiting for the same thing), a light situational opener follows naturally (see `conversation-and-wit.md`). If not — the smile itself was the win. No further action is required to "count."
**Root cause on the day it happened:** Recognized the moment, froze physically before any words were needed. See `breathing-and-nerves.md` — the fix was the physiological sigh + moving/holding eye contact, not a better line.

### Approaching someone at a bar/party
**Situation:** Wanting to approach someone you don't know in a social venue.
**Best response:** Approach directly, don't linger and build it up (lingering spikes the freeze response — see `breathing-and-nerves.md`, box breathing beforehand helps). Open with something situational about the venue/event, not a compliment. Stay a few minutes, read engagement, exit or escalate based on their response — don't overstay past a natural lull.

### Networking / professional introduction
**Situation:** Meeting someone at an event relevant to the AI consulting business.
**Best response:** Firm handshake, name + one clear line on what you do (not a pitch — "I build AI systems that handle lead response for trade businesses"). Ask what they do and actually follow up on the answer before pivoting to your own agenda. Business card / follow-up ask comes at a natural close, not mid-conversation.

### Client small talk (before/after a consulting conversation)
**Situation:** The few minutes of casual conversation around a business meeting.
**Best response:** Genuine, brief, situational (something about their business, the location, current events relevant to their industry). This is presence and composure practice as much as any dating scenario — same skills, different stakes.
