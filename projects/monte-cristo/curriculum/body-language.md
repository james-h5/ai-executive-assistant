# Body Language

## Posture (Standing)
- Weight evenly on both feet, spine long, shoulders back and *down* (not up around the ears — that's tension, not confidence).
- Chin level, not tilted up (arrogant) or down (avoidant).
- Chest open, not caved — caving the chest is one of the most common tells of a freeze/withdrawal state.

## Walk
- Even, unhurried pace. Rushing while walking reads as anxious or late-to-something even with a neutral face.
- Let arms swing naturally — don't pin them to the sides or cross them while moving.
- Eyes up, scanning the environment naturally — not locked on a phone, not staring at the ground. This is also what fixes "didn't register the moment until too late": if the eyes are down, the opportunity is invisible before it can even be acted on.

## Stance (Standing Still, e.g. in Conversation)
- Feet shoulder-width, weight settled — not shifting or rocking, which reads as impatience or nerves.
- Hands visible and relaxed — in pockets is fine (thumb out, not full fist-in-pocket which looks closed off), or loosely at sides, or gesturing naturally.
- Avoid full arm crossing in a conversation you want to go well — reads as closed even when not meant that way.

## Sitting
- Sit back into the chair, not perched on the edge (perching reads as ready to flee, i.e. still in freeze/flight state).
- Spread naturally into the space available rather than folding small — taking up appropriate space reads as ease, not entitlement, when paired with genuine attentiveness to the other person.

## Hands and Gestures
- Use hands naturally to support speech — stillness overcorrected into stiffness reads as tense, not composed.
- Avoid self-touch fidgeting (face-touching, hair, adjusting clothes repeatedly) — these spike when nervous and are one of the most visible tells.

## Eye Contact
- Hold eye contact through the end of a sentence, break naturally rather than darting away mid-thought.
- In a passing moment (like the train) — the ability to hold eye contact half a second longer than instinct wants, plus a genuine small smile, *is* the opener. It doesn't require words to not be a freeze.

## Debrief Questions
1. Where were the eyes — up and aware, or down/distracted, before the moment happened?
2. Any specific tell noticed after the fact (arms crossed, shifting weight, self-touch)?
3. Was the body's first reaction to move toward, away, or freeze in place?
