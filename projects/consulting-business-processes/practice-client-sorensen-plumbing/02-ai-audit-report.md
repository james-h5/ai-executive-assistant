## Summary
Rick runs the entire business — quotes, bookings, invoicing — off one mobile number and a
paper diary, with zero automation anywhere. The biggest, clearest opportunity is enquiry
handling: he estimates a few missed jobs a month from slow response alone, worth roughly
$1,600-2,400/month in recovered work at his average job value. Two smaller opportunities
(invoicing, review requests) are real but lower urgency.

## Opportunity 1: Enquiry Response & Qualification
- **Current state:** All enquiries go to one mobile number. If Rick's under a house or on a
  job, it rings out to voicemail. He checks and calls back "when he gets a chance" — often
  same evening, sometimes next afternoon.
- **The problem:** A handful of leads a month go quiet because the customer rings the next
  plumber down the list before Rick calls back. No way to know how many for certain, but at
  ~$400-800 per average job, even 2-3 lost jobs a month is $800-2,400 walking out the door.
- **Recommendation:** AI Lead Response + Follow-Up System — auto-replies within 60 seconds,
  asks qualifying questions (job type, location, urgency) for new contacts, recognizes
  repeat customers and skips straight to "notify Rick," and sends an automatic follow-up if
  the customer doesn't hear back within 24 hours.
- **Estimated impact:** 2-3 recovered jobs/month = ~$1,600-2,400/month.
- **Effort to implement:** Quick win (days) — this is a packaged offer, not custom-built from
  scratch.

## Opportunity 2: Invoicing
- **Current state:** Handwritten dockets on-site, invoices typed up later in a Word doc and
  emailed "when he remembers" — sometimes a week or two after the job.
- **The problem:** Delayed invoicing means delayed payment, and occasional forgotten invoices
  entirely. No record-keeping beyond the Word doc folder.
- **Recommendation:** Basic invoicing software (e.g. a simple app that generates and sends an
  invoice on the spot from the phone). Not an AI system — just standard tooling Rick hasn't
  adopted yet.
- **Estimated impact:** Faster payment cycle, fewer forgotten invoices. Hard to put a dollar
  figure on without more data.
- **Effort to implement:** Medium (weeks) — mostly a habit change, not a technical build; not
  in scope for James's core offer, worth a pointer/referral rather than a build.

## Opportunity 3: Review Requests
- **Current state:** Relies entirely on organic Google reviews — no system for asking happy
  customers to leave one.
- **The problem:** Word-of-mouth and reviews are his only marketing channel, and he's leaving
  easy reviews on the table by not asking.
- **Recommendation:** A simple automated text after job completion asking for a review
  (could piggyback on the same system built for Opportunity 1, once that's live).
- **Estimated impact:** Modest but compounding — more reviews, more inbound leads over time.
- **Effort to implement:** Quick win once Opportunity 1 exists — natural phase 2 add-on, not
  a separate build.
