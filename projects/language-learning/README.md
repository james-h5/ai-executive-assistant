# Language Learning

Build enough conversational ability in key languages to speak confidently with locals, make friends, and get by while travelling. Not fluency — just real-world usefulness.

**Status:** Active

## Description

James travels solo and wants to connect with locals rather than staying in the tourist bubble. The goal is to know enough of a language to order food, ask for directions, introduce himself, and have a basic conversation — enough to make someone's day and make a memory.

Use the `/learn-language` skill for practice sessions.

## Target Languages

| Language | Why | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Italian | Bucket list travel destination | High |
| Japanese | Loved Japan solo trip — wants to go deeper | High |
| French | Travel staple, widely useful | Medium |
| German | Europe travel | Medium |

## Scenarios to Cover (per language)
- Introductions — who you are, where you're from, what you do
- Ordering food and drinks
- Asking for directions
- Small talk — compliments, jokes, making friends
- At the bar / social situations
- Shopping and markets

## Next Steps
- Start first session with Italian or Japanese (whichever feels right that day)
- Build up scenario by scenario — no need to rush
