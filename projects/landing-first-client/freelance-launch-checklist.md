# Launch Checklist — Fiverr & Upwork

Copy is ready ([freelance-profile-copy.md](freelance-profile-copy.md)). This is the actual step-by-step to get live, since neither account exists yet.

## Day 1 — Fiverr signup + gig live

1. Go to fiverr.com → Sign Up (email or Google) → select **"Become a Seller"**
2. Fill out Seller Profile (this is your personal bio, separate from the gig description):
   - Profile photo — a real photo of yourself, not a logo (buyers trust faces)
   - Professional description — 2-3 sentences, can reuse the "Why me" line from the gig copy
   - Skills — add: Prompt Engineering, AI Development, Business Automation, Zapier
   - Education — QUT, Engineering (Electrical) + Business (Finance)
   - Language — English (Native/Fluent)
3. Create your first Gig: Selling → Gigs → Create a New Gig
   - Category: Programming & Tech → AI Services → AI Applications (pick closest match Fiverr offers)
   - Paste in the title, description, and 3 packages from `freelance-profile-copy.md`
   - Gig gallery: needs at least 1 image. Make a simple Canva graphic (title + "Claude AI Automation" + your name) if you don't have real work samples yet — swap in real screenshots once you deliver your first job
   - FAQs: paste in from the copy doc
   - Requirements: add 1-2 questions buyers must answer when ordering (e.g. "What process do you want automated?")
4. Publish. Fiverr gigs often take a few hours to become searchable — normal, don't resubmit.

**Cost:** Free to join. Fiverr takes 20% commission on every order — price accordingly (already factored into the package prices in the copy doc).

## Day 1-2 — Upwork signup + profile live

1. Go to upwork.com → Sign Up → select **"I'm a freelancer, looking for work"**
2. Complete profile:
   - Photo + title + overview from `freelance-profile-copy.md`
   - Skills: add all 8 from the copy doc
   - Employment/education history: add QUT (Engineering + Business, in progress) — Upwork lets you show "still studying," that's fine
   - Hourly rate: start at $50-75/hr per the copy doc guidance
   - Portfolio: skip for now or add 1 placeholder ("AI automation demo" screenshot) — replace with real work after job #1
3. Identity verification: Upwork will ask for ID verification before you can apply to jobs — have a driver's license or passport ready, usually approved within a day
4. Profile goes into Upwork's review queue — approval usually takes 24-48 hours before it's fully live and searchable

**Cost / Connects:** Upwork gives a limited number of free "Connects" per month (used to submit proposals — roughly 10-16 free/month depending on account). Each proposal costs a few Connects. If you run out, buying more is optional (~$0.15 each) — don't buy a big pack until you've tested how many proposals it takes to land a reply.

## First week — get moving

- [ ] Fiverr gig published and searchable
- [ ] Upwork profile submitted for review
- [ ] Once Upwork's approved: apply to 3-5 jobs/day using the proposal template, prioritizing small/quick jobs (prompt writing, small automations) to build reviews fast
- [ ] Log every gig post + proposal sent in [freelance-pipeline.md](freelance-pipeline.md)
- [ ] First job that comes in: prioritize a 5-star review over maximizing price
- [ ] After 2-3 completed jobs: swap the placeholder gig image for a real screenshot/result

## Notes
- Don't wait for the "perfect" profile — a live, imperfect gig beats a polished draft. Iterate after the first few views/orders.
- If Fiverr/Upwork reject or flag anything during signup (happens sometimes for new accounts), it's usually a photo or category mismatch — fix and resubmit, not a sign to give up on the channel.
