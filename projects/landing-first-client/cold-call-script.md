# Cold Call Discovery Script

Purpose: find out what these businesses actually have (if anything) handling enquiries, and where the gap really is — before assuming or redesigning the offer. This is a discovery call, not a pitch call. Don't sell on the first call.

## Opening (10-15 seconds, low pressure)

> "Hey, is this [Business Name]? ... Hey [Name if known], no worries if it's a bad time — I'll be quick. I'm James, I help local trade businesses with the way they handle incoming enquiries. Got 60 seconds?"

If no: "No dramas — is there a better time to catch you?" Then hang up, don't push.

## Discovery questions (the actual point of the call)

Ask in order, let them talk, don't rush to the pitch:

1. "When someone calls or messages you for a quote and you're on a job or after hours — what happens to that enquiry right now?"
2. "Do you use anything automatic for that — like a chatbot, auto-reply, or booking system?"
   - If **yes**: "Oh nice, what does it actually do — does it just send a message back, or does it ask questions and let you know straight away?" (this is the key question — most "chatbots" are a generic auto-reply, not a qualifying/notifying system)
   - If **no**: "Right, so if someone messages at 6pm tonight, when would they actually hear back?"
3. "Roughly how many enquiries would you say you miss or lose because of the timing?" (rough number is fine, don't push for precision)
4. "What about anywhere else in the business — scheduling, invoicing, marketing, follow-ups — anything automated or AI-assisted there?" (one open question, not a checklist — this is market intelligence for you, let them talk, don't itemize every department)

## What you're listening for

Three outcomes, each with a different next move:

- **Genuine gap in message-response specifically** (no automation, or a generic "thanks we'll be in touch" auto-reply with no qualifying/notification) → real prospect for the core offer, move to **Soft close A**
- **No AI anywhere in the business** (not even elsewhere — scheduling, invoicing, marketing) → bigger opportunity than a demo link, move to **Soft close B**
- **Real AI system already in place** (qualifies the lead, notifies the owner instantly, handles follow-up — or genuinely solid automation elsewhere) → thank them, note it, don't pitch — this business already solved the problem
- **Genuine emergency-only business** you missed in the lead-gen filter → note it, deprioritize

## Soft close A — message-response gap only

> "That's pretty much exactly what I help with — I build a system that auto-replies within 60 seconds, asks the right questions, and pings you straight away so nothing sits there. Would it be worth me sending you a quick demo to look at when you've got a sec? No pressure either way."

If yes: get their preferred contact (SMS/email/Instagram) and send the demo link after the call — don't try to close on the phone.
If hesitant: "All good, I'll leave you to it — appreciate you taking the call."

## Soft close B — no AI anywhere (book a sit-down)

This is the better outcome — a business with zero AI adoption anywhere has more to gain than just the message-response fix, and it's worth more of your time than a demo link.

> "Honestly, sounds like there's a fair bit that could save you time, not just the enquiry side. Would you be open to me swinging by for 20-30 minutes sometime — no cost, no obligation — just to have a look at where AI could actually help across the business? Worst case you get a few free ideas."

If yes: lock in a day/time on the spot if you can, otherwise get their preferred contact to confirm.
If hesitant: "No worries at all — I'll leave the offer open, give me a shout if that changes."
